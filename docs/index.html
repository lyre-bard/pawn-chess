📜 Pawn Chess Rules

  • Objective: First player to safely escape 2 pieces through the opponent's baseline wins.
  • Movement: EVERY piece on the board moves exactly 1 square at a time.
  • Pawns: Move straight forward, attack diagonally. Any unmoved backline pawn can move 2 squares on their very first move. If frontally locked, can move diagonally.
  • Pawn Opening Layouts:
    • Zigzag Open (Default): Alternating zigzag pattern on rows 1 & 2.
    • Front Row Only: All 8 pawns deployed on row 1 (closest to center).
    • Back Row Only: All 8 pawns deployed on row 2 (in front of backline).
    • Random Open: Pawns randomly distributed between rows 1 & 2.
  • Backline Pieces (R, N, B, Q, K): Move 1 square diagonally. They are color-locked to their initial tiles.
  • Straight Attacks & Color Shifts:
    • R, N, B can capture their exact counterpart straight ahead.
    • Queens can capture any backline piece straight ahead (cannot attack Pawns straight ahead).
    • Kings can capture Queens and Kings straight ahead.
    • When capturing straight ahead, the piece moves forward and dynamically changes its color property to the new tile.
